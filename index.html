How I think
"Great storytelling leads with the conviction behind a product, not its features."— Raman Vedula
01
One visceral "So What"
Every piece of content has a single thesis that permanently reframes how the audience sees something. Not a list of takeaways. One frame shift. Everything else is logistics.
02
Never an event recap
A story is a provocation, not a summary. If a reader can get the same thing from a news ticker, the job isn't done. The goal is always the version that changes the question.
03
AI as editorial infrastructure
AI doesn't replace editorial instinct, it multiplies it. The organizations that win will combine genuine storytelling craft with AI architecture. That system has already been built.
